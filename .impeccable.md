# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Stuart Grant (principal agent) and an admin/PA who handles data entry. Stuart reviews and approves. Usage is recurring — primarily weekly, before vendor calls. The admin enters portal stats and inspection data; Stuart generates reports and sends vendor links. Both users are professionals who work quickly and expect the tool to keep up.

### Brand Personality
GEA is a Melbourne boutique agency. The internal tool should feel: **premium, editorial, sharp**. Like a well-designed financial terminal crossed with a luxury property magazine back-office. It should feel distinctly GEA — not generic SaaS, not a consumer app.

Three words: **Authoritative. Refined. Efficient.**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — used in a bright office environment during business hours
- **Feel**: High-end editorial. Think FT Weekend property section, Wallpaper* CMS, or a luxury broker's internal system. Dense with information but never cluttered. Sharp geometry, not bubbly.
- **Anti-references**: Consumer apps (rounded-everything, pastel, big friendly CTAs), generic SaaS dashboards (dark sidebar + metric card grid), REA/Domain portal aesthetics
- **Brand colours**: Navy `#1e3a5f` and gold `#c9a96e` are the GEA brand — they should inform the palette but the internal tool need not use them as heavily as vendor-facing pages

### Design Principles
1. **Information over decoration** — every element earns its place by communicating something useful
2. **Sharp, not soft** — geometry should be crisp. Radius reserved for intentional moments, not applied uniformly to every card
3. **Density with hierarchy** — agents need to scan many properties quickly. Information should be dense but scannable via strong hierarchy
4. **Distinct from the vendor portal** — the internal tool and the vendor-facing portal should look clearly different; agents should feel the shift when switching contexts
5. **Speed-first interactions** — data entry and approval flows should be frictionless, optimised for the weekly recurring workflow
